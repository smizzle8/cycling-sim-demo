Cycling Sim — Trial Build

G2 hardware · G1 trial logic · G3 instrumentation.

Trial

Track: straights + signed turns with curvature; arrow cue 30 m before each turn.

World rotates: as the track curves, the road bends on screen — match it with steer.

Stop: auto at finish line, or 3:00 timeout.

Logged @10 Hz: t, pid, cond, speed, cadence, steer, dist, lat_offset, segment, cue, brake, off_road.

Hardware Concept

Three signals are enough. Keep it dumb, keep it cheap.

Reed switch + wheel magnet longitudinal$2
10kΩ pot on stem lateral$2
Brake-lever microswitch$1
Arduino Nano (USB-HID)$5
Wires, zip-ties, glue$1
Total BOM~$11

Nano emulates a USB gamepad: cadence + steer as axes, brake as button. Browser sees it natively.

Experiment Notes

Design: within-subjects, 3 conditions × counter-balanced order, 2 trials each.

Comparable to: de Winter et al. (bike-sim validity); O'Hern bicycle simulator review.

Extra parameters: cue reaction time, lateral RMS error, off-road duration, brake events, steer-reversal rate.

Subjective: NASA-TLX, SSQ (sim sickness), single-item realism.
