Cycling Sim Demo

Keyboard now — cheap hardware later.

Hardware Concept

Three signals are enough for a convincing ride. Keep it dumb, keep it cheap.

Reed switch + wheel magnet longitudinal$2
10kΩ potentiometer on stem lateral$2
Brake-lever microswitch$1
Arduino Nano clone (USB HID)$5
Wires, zip-ties, hot-glue$1
Total BOM~$11

No smart trainer, no VR headset, no proprietary protocol. Mounts on any bike on any trainer — or even a stationary stand. The Nano emulates a USB gamepad so the browser sees pedal cadence, steer angle, and brake as standard axes/buttons.

Why this works

Pedal pulses → speed integration. Pot voltage → steer angle. Brake closes a contact. The sim does the physics; the bike just reports what the rider is doing.
